Associate Professor

Jie Ren

Compilers, Binary Analysis, Edge AI, and Mobile Systems
School of Artificial Intelligence and Computer Science, Shaanxi Normal University

I work on efficient computing for resource-constrained systems, with interests spanning program optimization, binary analysis and decompilation, mobile and edge AI, and large-model inference efficiency.

My goal is to build practical systems that connect compiler techniques, machine learning, and real-world deployment on modern platforms.

Research

A concise overview of my main research directions and current focus.

Program Optimization and Compilers

Compiler optimization, LLVM-based analysis, iterative tuning, and performance portability across platforms.

Binary Analysis and Decompilation

Recovering compiler-friendly IR from binaries for software analysis, migration, reverse engineering, and verification.

Edge AI and Efficient Inference

Lightweight deployment, low-latency inference, and efficient training strategies for large models on edge devices.

Mobile Systems

Energy-efficient mobile computing, adaptive offloading, and system support for high-performance intelligent applications.
